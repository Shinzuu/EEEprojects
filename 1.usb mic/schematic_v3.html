DIY USB Mic — Schematic v3 (noisy-room build, single 20 mm omni)

20 mm raw 2-terminal back-electret capsule (omni, confirmed by analog-meter test) → JFET buffer (bootstrapped) → 100 Hz HPF → NE5532 preamp → JFET-VCA gate (tightened threshold) → NE5532 buffer → 3.5 mm TRS into existing CM108-class USB dongle. BOM uses parts already on order from udvabony.com. No new parts needed.

audio-grade signal path ±9 V isolated rails 12-pos rotary gain v3: 100 Hz HPF for traffic rumble v3: NE5532 sidechain (TL072 not stocked)

Changes vs v2:
  1. Sidechain rebuilt around NE5532 (not TL072). TL072 was dropped from the finalized udvabony cart on 2026-05-01; NE5532 is what arrived. Bipolar input means we lose the JFET-input bias-current advantage, but with 22 k feedback resistors (not 1 M) the bias-current offset stays under 1 mV — fine for a comparator/envelope.
  2. New 100 Hz Sallen-Key HPF between Stage 1 and Stage 2. Targets the dominant noise source for this build environment (Dhaka traffic + AC compressor + ceiling-fan low-frequency rumble). 12 dB/oct rolloff below 100 Hz preserves voice fundamentals (80–250 Hz dips slightly but proximity effect at 3–5 cm restores it).
  3. Gate threshold range tightened from −60…−20 dB sweep to −40…−10 dB sweep. In a noisy room you want the gate biased to close on anything quieter than the voice; sweeping all the way to −60 dB never closes.
  4. Asymmetric attack/release kept from v2 — fast attack (~5 ms), slow release (~150 ms) so the gate doesn't chatter on consonants.
  5. Capsule confirmed raw, no internal FET (analog meter, ×10k range, ~1.5 MΩ symmetric both directions = capacitor + bleed resistor, no junction). v2's bootstrapped JFET front-end is the correct topology and is kept verbatim.
Why omni in a noisy room is still a compromise: a single omnidirectional capsule cannot reject off-axis sound. The real noise rejection in this build comes from (a) close-mic technique — voice 3–5 cm from the capsule, room noise unchanged, ~20 dB free SNR — (b) the 100 Hz HPF killing low-frequency traffic energy, and (c) the noise gate silencing the room when you stop talking. If your environment is consistently louder than ~55 dB SPL during recording, build a gradient cardioid pair instead (separate v3-grad spec, not this document).

1. Power architecture (unchanged from v2)

You have isolated DC-DC modules in 5 V / 9 V / 12 V single-rail. Use two single-rail 5 V → 9 V isolators to synthesize ±9 V:

USB 5V VBUS ──┬── Isolator A: 5V → 9V  ──── +9V rail (audio side)
              └── Isolator B: 5V → 9V (output reference flipped) ──── −9V rail
                  ↑ wire B's "+" output as common audio ground, "−" as −9V

Both modules MUST be galvanically isolated — otherwise the inversion floats audio ground from USB ground and you get a hum loop. Decouple each rail with 470 µF electrolytic + 100 nF ceramic at the preamp end of the cable.

The USB sound dongle runs from its own USB 5 V (do not share with the audio rails). The dongle's analog GND meets your audio GND only at the 3.5 mm TRS sleeve — single point.

Why this works (recap from v2): R3 + R4 form a 20 MΩ DC return to ground for the gate. C_boot drives the R3/R4 junction with the source signal, which (because Av ≈ 0.85 on a source-follower) makes the AC voltage across R3 only ~15 % of the gate signal — so the effective AC impedance R3 sees is R3 / (1 − Av) ≈ 10M / 0.15 ≈ 67M. With ~50 pF capsule, LF corner ≈ 48 Hz. Below the HPF cutoff anyway, so OK.

Bias check (2N3819, Vp ≈ −3 V, Idss ≈ 4 mA typ)

R2 = 4.7 k → Id ≈ 2.5 mA → Vs ≈ +2.7 V (above audio GND). Vd = +9 − (2.5 mA × 4.7 k) = −2.7 V → wait — drain sits at +9V − 11.75 V. That puts Vd below GND. Re-checking…

Correct: with R1 = 4.7 k, Id = 2.5 mA → drop across R1 = 11.75 V, but supply is only +9 V. JFET cannot pass 2.5 mA — it pinches off earlier. Real Id with this setup is Id ≈ (9 − Vs) / (R1 + R2) = 9 / 9.4k = 0.96 mA. So Vs ≈ +0.45 V (1 mA × 4.7k / 10), Vd ≈ +9 − 4.5 = +4.5 V, Vds ≈ 4 V. JFET operates in saturation, ~4 V headroom each way. Same outcome as v2 numerically, just my arithmetic above was sloppy — the actual operating point self-arranges via Idss-Vp curve.

Sample variance: 2N3819 Idss ranges 2–20 mA across samples. Plan to measure Vs after build and adjust R2 to put Vs in 0.5–1.5 V window. Spare 2N3819 stock is ample.

4. Stage 2 — 100 Hz high-pass filter NEW

Sallen-Key 2-pole high-pass topology, NE5532/A:

   in (from C7 1µF) ──► C20 100nF ──┬──► C21 100nF ──┬──► non-inv input NE5532/A
                                     │                 │
                                     R20 16k           R21 16k
                                     │                 │
                                    GND               output
                                                       │
                                          inv input ◄──┤
                                                       │
                                                       ▼
                                                   to Stage 3 input

Filter math

  • fc = 1 / (2π × √(R20 × R21 × C20 × C21)) = 1 / (2π × √(16k × 16k × 100n × 100n)) = 99.5 Hz ✓
  • Q = √(R20 × R21 × C20 × C21) / (R20 × C20 + R20 × C21) = 0.5 with these values — slightly Bessel-flat, no peaking
  • Stop-band: −12 dB at 50 Hz, −24 dB at 25 Hz, −40 dB at 10 Hz

Why 100 Hz, not 80 Hz: Dhaka traffic noise spectrum peaks at 50–80 Hz (truck idling, 2-stroke rickshaw exhaust). 80 Hz cutoff lets that through. 100 Hz puts the noise in the −3 dB region, voice fundamentals (male 85–180 Hz, female 165–255 Hz) lose the bottom 1–2 dB but proximity effect at 3–5 cm distance restores 4–6 dB at 100 Hz, net positive.

Why Sallen-Key, not passive RC: 2-pole gives 12 dB/oct rolloff vs 6 dB/oct passive. Passive would still leak 30 % of 50 Hz energy through; this kills it. Cost: one NE5532 half (we have spare).

NE5532 as comparator caveat: NE5532 is not a true comparator; output recovers slowly from rail saturation (~10 µs). For audio gating this is fine — voice envelopes change on ms timescales. Do not use NE5532 as comparator if you need <1 µs response.

Why R-values are smaller than the v2 TL072 design: NE5532 input bias current is ~200 nA (vs ~30 pA on TL072). With a 1 MΩ feedback that's 200 mV of offset — would jam the comparator one way. With 22 k feedback it's 4.4 mV — negligible.

Threshold range: VR1 wiper sweeps from −6 V to −1 V at the comparator's inverting input, mapping to gate-closed thresholds of roughly −40 dB (mostly closed) to −10 dB (rarely closed). Tighter range than v2 because in a noisy room you never want the gate trying to discriminate −60 dB signals.

Asymmetric attack/release: 1N4148 in parallel with R22, anode toward C32 — when envelope rising (attack), diode forward-biases, charges C32 quickly through ~100 Ω wire resistance (5 ms time constant). When envelope falling (release), diode blocks, C32 discharges through R22 = 10 kΩ → 100 ms time constant. Smooth release prevents gate chatter on consonants.

  MUTE LED branch (red, lights when muted):
   +5V ── 1kΩ ── LED_red anode
                 LED_red cathode ──┐

   When SW1 OPEN: cathode floats (audio signal node, AC, no DC path) → LED off
   When SW1 CLOSED: cathode reaches AGND through switch → LED on (red), audio shorted

  POWER LED branch (green, always on):
   +5V ── 1kΩ ── LED_green anode ── LED_green cathode ── AGND
   Always lit when powered. Independent of mute state.
Why the mute switch is BEFORE C40, not after: the buffer (NE5532/F) is happy to drive 220 Ω + dead-short to AGND — well within its 38 mA short-circuit current rating. Placing SW1 here means C40 sees 220 Ω source impedance at all times. When mute toggles, C40 doesn't have to charge/discharge to a new voltage rail — the audio node just goes to 0 V. No DC thump on press / release.

Why the red LED shares SW1's contact: simplest possible. One SPST switch does both the audio mute AND the LED indicator through a single contact. When the switch closes, both:
  • the audio node shorts to AGND (silence)
  • the red LED's cathode reaches AGND (LED lights)
No separate logic, no DPDT switch needed. (DPDT works too — with that, pole 1 = audio, pole 2 = LED, two independent circuits.)

Switch type: use a latching push-button (push-on, push-off) or a slide switch. Tact switches (B3F-4055 from your cart) are momentary — they only mute while held. For latching behavior with a tact switch you'd need a flip-flop chip; not worth the complexity.

USB sound card: any cheap CM108-class adapter from your bench. Critical mod: open the dongle, find the bias resistor that feeds +5 V (or +2.5 V) to the mic-input pin (usually 2.2 k labelled R13 or similar), desolder it. Otherwise the dongle pushes phantom-ish bias voltage into your buffer's output, which (a) wastes headroom and (b) can damage NE5532's output driver if it's a hard short to +5 V.

Use the dongle's mic-input level (sensitive, ~10 mV reference) — your buffer outputs ~50–500 mV depending on rotary position. For louder source / less dongle noise, consider feeding the dongle's line-in if present (most CM108 dongles only expose mic-in; some boards have line-in pads).

11. Build & bring-up notes

  1. Verify ±9 V rails first with no ICs in sockets. Both isolators powered, decoupling caps in place, measure +9.0 ±0.3 V and −9.0 ±0.3 V at NE5532 socket pin 8 / pin 4 respectively. If either rail is off, fix before installing ICs.
  2. First-power smoke test: 100 Ω resistor in series with each rail temporarily — if anything glows, the resistor saves the DC-DC modules.
  3. Bring up Stage 1 alone: install Q1, capsule attached. Probe Vs (Q1 source). Should be 0.3–1.5 V above audio GND. If Vs > 2 V, swap Q1 (high Idss sample) or raise R2. If Vs < 0.2 V, low-Idss sample, lower R2 to 2.2 k.
  4. Inject test tone: 1 kHz, 1 mV-pp via 100 pF cap into capsule (+) terminal. Should see ~0.85 mV-pp at Q1 source (Av ≈ 0.85). Confirm before moving on.
  5. Bring up Stage 2 (HPF): install one NE5532. Sweep tone 30 Hz to 300 Hz at constant amplitude. Verify −12 dB at 50 Hz, −3 dB at 100 Hz, flat above 200 Hz.
  6. Bring up Stage 3 (preamp): rotary at pos 4 (R8 = 4.7k → +14 dB). Inject 1 mV at HPF input, expect 5 mV at preamp output.
  7. Bring up Stage 5 (sidechain) WITHOUT Stage 4 connected: tap audio at preamp output, watch comparator output on scope. Speak into mic — comparator should swing −9 V → +9 V on syllables. Adjust VR1 until threshold sits ~10 dB below normal speaking level.
  8. Connect Stage 4 (VCA): with sidechain working, the gate should now open on speech and close in silence. Listen for chatter; if present, increase release time-constant (larger C32).
  9. Connect dongle: only after all the above passes. Use shortest possible 3.5 mm cable (10–20 cm). Confirm host sees the dongle as a USB Audio Class device. Record a test in Audacity, check noise floor in silence — should be < −60 dB FS at preamp pos 8.

12. Mechanical & acoustic notes

  • Capsule mounting: shock-isolate via foam grommet or rubber band suspension. Mechanical noise from a desk hit will conduct through the chassis to the capsule otherwise.
  • Capsule wiring: enameled copper wire run inside copper desoldering braid (you have braid from the DIY Perks build). Braid grounded to chassis at capsule end ONLY — single-point shield ground prevents ground loops.
  • Gate node hygiene: the Q1 gate / R3 / R4 / capsule junction is gigaohm-class. PCB needs a guard ring around it tied to Q1 source (the bootstrap node). No flux residue. Conformal coat after build is recommended for humidity (Dhaka monsoon).
  • Enclosure: closed metal box (aluminium tea-tin, project box, even a metal cookie tin). Front face has the capsule + foam windscreen; back face has the rotary, threshold pot, and 3.5 mm output. Chassis is the shield — tie chassis to audio GND at the 3.5 mm sleeve.
  • Windscreen: open-cell foam in front of the capsule, ~10 mm thick. Cuts plosives and breath bursts. Cheap upholstery foam or a salvaged pop-filter works.
  • Recording position: mic 3–5 cm from corner of mouth, slightly off-axis (say, 30°). Off-axis position avoids the worst plosives. Close distance gives 20+ dB SNR over room noise — this is the single biggest noise reducer in the build, more than HPF or gate combined.
