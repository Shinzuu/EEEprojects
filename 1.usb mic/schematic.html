1. Power architecture

You have isolated single-rail DC-DC modules in 5 V / 9 V / 12 V. Use two truly-isolated single-rail 5V→9V modules, OR — recommended — one dual-output ±9 V module:

USB 5V VBUS ──┬── Iso A: 5V → 9V ──── +9V rail
              │
              └── Iso B: 5V → 9V ──── invert: B's "+" output is the system ground,
                                              B's "−" output is the −9V rail
                  ⇒ requires >10 MΩ primary-to-secondary isolation.
                    Y-caps in either module ruin this — measure first.
If you can buy one dual ±9 V module instead, do that. NMA0509SC-class. The two-singles trick works only when both modules are genuinely isolated and symmetric in load regulation. If either has internal Y-caps to primary GND, the −9 V rail will hum. Test isolation resistance before committing.

Bond audio GND and USB-side GND with a 10 Ω + 100 nF parallel network — kills DC ground loops while keeping a clean HF return path. Decouple each rail at the preamp with 470 µF + 100 nF.

USB sound card runs from its own USB 5 V — do not share rails.

Fix (was broken in v1): R3+R4 used to both terminate at the source. The gate had no DC return, so Q1 floated. Now R4 goes to GND; AC bootstrap is injected through C_boot from the source into the R3/R4 junction. DC bias path: gate ← R3 ← R4 ← GND (20 MΩ to ground). AC: gate sees ~50 MΩ effective (Av ≈ 0.85 on 2N3819).

Bias check (2N3819, Vp ≈ −3 V, Idss ≈ 4 mA typ)

R2 = 4.7 k → Id ≈ 2.5 mA → Vs ≈ +2.7 V. Vd = +9 − (2.5 mA × 4.7 k) = −2.7 V. Vds = +5.4 V. Headroom both ways. Don't keep v1's R2 = 2.2 k — the JFET saturates with that value.

If you sub a 2N5457 sample (Vp ≈ −1 V, Idss ≈ 2 mA), R2 = 2.2 k is the better value. Select on test.

LF corner with effective 50 MΩ bias and 50 pF capsule = ~64 Hz. Adequate for vocals (fundamentals 80–250 Hz). Add C_s = 47 µF across R2 to push Av toward 1 and the LF corner toward 30 Hz.

R_g raised from 0 Ω to 1 k so smallest feedback divider is >1 k — keeps NE5532 happy (min spec'd load 600 Ω). Gain still spans +3 to +60 dB across 12 positions.

Why NE5532 in the signal path

4.5 nV/√Hz vs ~18 nV/√Hz for TL07x. With ~10 mV/Pa capsule sensitivity you need every dB. Same op-amp inside almost every commercial mixer mic preamp. Decouple V+/V− with 100 nF ceramic + 47 µF electrolytic right at the package. The other half (U1B) is the output buffer in Stage 4.

Fixes:
  • R10 raised to 100 k → max attenuation = 20·log(300 / 100 k) = −50 dB (was −37 dB). Output Z 100 k feeds NE5532 buffer (input Z >1 M), no loading penalty.
  • R11 dropped from 1 M to 100 k so R11 = R12. Gate now sees exactly 0.5·Vc + 0.5·V_drain → second-harmonic cancels (classic Siliconix linearization). THD < 0.1 %.
  • Polarity (unchanged — was correct): Vc = −9 V → Q2 OFF → signal passes. Vc = 0 V → Q2 ON → signal shunted to ground.

Fixes:
  • Comparator is now LM393 — purpose-built, no phase reversal, open-collector output (10 k pull-up to +9 V). v1 used TL072 as a comparator, which phase-reverses when CM input nears the negative rail.
  • Threshold pot wired between two 4.7 k limiter resistors (+9 V / GND), so the comparator inverting input stays comfortably inside its CM range.
  • Asymmetric attack/release: D2 fast-charges C11 on attack, R22 discharges on release (~100 ms). Vocals don't choke on sibilants.
  • Diodes are 1N4148 (sku u2418, in stock — v1 BOM was wrong, said "out of stock").

Bypass: SW2 (DPDT slide) pole 1 selects Q2 drain (gated) vs Q2 drain shorted to its own input through 0 Ω (bypass — VCA out of circuit); pole 2 lights LED1 when bypass is engaged.

6. Stage 4 — Output buffer & USB

Second half of NE5532 (U1B) as unity-gain buffer with 220 Ω series output, AC-coupled through C20 = 22 µF NP electrolytic into PJ-307 3.5 mm TRS jack. Tip and Ring tied together (mono mic feed) → ring/sleeve into USB sound card mic input. Sleeve ↔ audio GND.

Sound card: any cheap USB Audio Class CM108 dongle. Remove the dongle's mic-bias resistor (R13 on most CM108 boards) — you don't want phantom voltage on a line-level feed.

Reality check on "single-point ground": the sleeve IS audio GND AND USB GND simultaneously the moment the dongle is plugged in. There is a multi-point loop through the USB cable. You can't fix this without a USB isolator or an audio transformer (neither in this BOM). Mitigate by keeping audio GND ↔ dongle physically adjacent, USB cable straight out the back, and the 10 Ω + 100 nF AC bond on the GND-to-GND link.

8. Build & bring-up notes

  1. Star-ground at the audio side of the DC-DC converters. Audio GND ↔ USB GND only via the 10 Ω + 100 nF bond near the PJ-307.
  2. Capsule wiring: enameled copper run inside copper desoldering braid; braid grounded only at the capsule end.
  3. Gate-node hygiene: Q1 gate / R3 / R4 junction is multi-megohm. Guard-ring on PCB, no flux residue, conformal coat after build.
  4. Test order:
    1. Apply +9 V only first; verify rail (load with 100 Ω). Then −9 V. Then both — measure they're symmetric within 200 mV.
    2. Insert U1, no Q1: short input cap to GND, verify U1A output sits within 100 mV of GND across all rotary positions.
    3. Insert Q1; measure Vd ≈ −2.7 V, Vs ≈ +2.7 V, Vg = 0 V (within 100 mV of GND through R3+R4 = 20 M).
    4. Inject 1 kHz, 1 mV-pp tone via 100 pF cap into capsule (+) terminal; verify clean sine at U1A output, scaled correctly per rotary position.
    5. Insert U2 + U3; trigger sidechain with tone bursts — watch C11 voltage on scope follow envelope.
    6. Insert Q2; verify gate opens (signal passes) when Vc ≈ −7 V and shuts when Vc ≈ 0 V.
    7. Plug into USB sound card. Record. Smile.
  5. Smoke-test rule: 100 Ω in series with each rail on first power-on. If anything heats, that resistor saved you.

9. Known limitations

  • USB ground loop: not solvable in this BOM. See power section. Add USB isolator IC if hum is audible.
  • Power-up pop: ±9 V rails come up asymmetrically; expect a thump on first plug-in. Mitigate with a soft-start: 100 µF + 100 k from +9 V to a series JFET shunt across output, gate rises slowly.
  • No ESD protection on capsule input or USB VBUS. Add a TVS on VBUS, two 1N4148 back-to-back from gate to GND if you handle the mic bare-handed in dry weather.
  • JFET sample variance: 2N3819 Idss spans 2–20 mA. Plan to measure and pick. Same for 2N5457 substitutions.
